| Scotland |
Glenfiddich

A colossus, Glenfiddich was established by William Grant in 1876, with the first spirit running off the stills in 1887. Today the company is still run by his descendants and as the first whiskey to truly market itself as a single malt, Glenfiddich now accounts for about 30% of all single malt sales worldwide.

| Scotland |
The Glenlivet

One of the most famous malts in the world, perhaps on account of the story of George Smith,an illegal distiller in Speyside who went legit when he obtained the first licence to legally distill whiskey in 1824.

| Ireland |
Jameson

The most popular Irish whiskey in the world, with more than 20 million bottles sold per year, all over the globe. Produced at the Midleton distillery, it is the flagship whiskey of Irish Distillers.

| Ireland |
Bushmills

Whiskey making at Bushmills draws on centuries-old distilling history, including a royal licence to distil whiskey, granted for the county of Antrim in 1608.

| United States |
Woodford Reserve

Distillation began on site of what is now the Woodford Reserve distillery in Versailles in 1812, which makes it the oldest operating distillery in Kentucky, although this operation has not been continuous: the distillery was closed for around thirty years from the 1960s to the mid-1990s.

| United States |
Jack Daniel's

Jack Daniel's is a brand of Tennessee whiskey and the top-selling American whiskey in the world. It is produced in Lynchburg, Tennessee, by the Jack Daniel Distillery, which has been owned by the Brown-Forman Corporation since 1956.

| Japan |
Nikka

Founded in 1934 by Suntory's first master distiller, Nikka has risen to become Japan's second largest distiller, producing a wide range of single malts as well as their impressive blended whiskey range.

| Japan |
Yamazaki

Back in 1923, the Vale of Yamazaki was chosen as the place where he would build whiskey distillery for its supply of good water and a favourable climate for maturation. Yamazaki is now home to 12 stills of three different varieties. This allows them to create whiskies with different flavour profiles, which is useful for blending.

| Taiwan |
Kavalan

It was back in 2008 that Taiwan’s King Car Group conglomerate released the first Kavalan whiskey, inspired by the country’s relatively huge consumption of Scotch in recent years. Since then, the company hasn’t looked back, combining a fruit-centred spirit style with multiple cask types, a willingness to experiment and the rapid maturation encouraged by Taiwan’s tropical climate.
